These are a bunch of obtuse questions I forced Sam Irwin to answer.
It was in exchange for my answers so it's not like it was under duress.
No Sam's were harmed during this interview.

November Something Something, 2019

Why do you want to be a designer?

I want to be a designer because it's a career that allows me to express some artistic ability while also making things for people.

Why are you here?

Why are any of us really here?

What worries/excites you about being a designer?

I worry about my own ability to create good designs that people will like. I'm excited about getting to create cool designs for people and companies all around the world someday, hopefully.

What aspect of design do you gravitate towards?

I enjoy getting to create more illustrative designs, and less those with photography or strongly featuring type.

What is difficult in design, how do you push past that?

I tend to struggle more with typography, I struggle applying design principles to just the letter forms.

Can design change the world?

Design is everywhere. Without design people wouldn't know what exit to take on the highway or what any business' are. Design can have a huge influence on people all over the world, and so yes, it can change the world.

What role or responsibility do you think a designer plays in our society?

I think that it's the responsibility to try and steer clients towards good, functional designs that will benefit society.

What do you have in your future?

Nothing.
